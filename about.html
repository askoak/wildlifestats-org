About WildlifeStats

WildlifeStats is a national research framework for the analysis of wildlife rehabilitation patient data. Rehabilitation centers record detailed information about the animals they admit — species, the reason for admission, geography, season, and outcome — but those records sit in incompatible systems and rarely reach the researchers, educators, and policy analysts who could use them.

The framework standardizes those records into a single queryable structure and publishes aggregate findings at national scale. It is built for researchers studying wildlife health and human-wildlife interaction, for policy analysts and conservation organizations, and for educators who need accessible, well-documented data.

The dataset currently shown is synthetic — 100,000 records generated from regional distribution models — and is labeled as such wherever it appears. It demonstrates the framework's methodology while a real multi-center dataset is assembled under the tiered access model described under Governance.
