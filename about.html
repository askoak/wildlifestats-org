About

About WildlifeStats

What this is

WildlifeStats is a national research framework for wildlife rehabilitation, disease, injury, and One Health data. The framework provides a synthetic reference dataset, a searchable national database surface, a triage and referral assistant (Wildlife911) for finders of injured wildlife, and a methodology pipeline that would aggregate heterogeneous wildlife rehabilitation records into a common schema if real partner data were contributed under partner agreements.

Who it is for

The framework serves a range of users with different needs: wildlife disease researchers and biostatisticians who want micro-data and reproducible queries; state wildlife agency biologists who want their region in national context; partner wildlife rehabilitation organizations who want anonymized peer comparison; clinical practitioners and educators who want institutional reference content; finders of injured wildlife who want a calm, accurate first step. The site is designed so the same surfaces serve all of them without a separate “kids’ mode” or “expert mode” — progressive disclosure does the work.

What this is not

WildlifeStats is not a real-time surveillance system, not a clinical triage dispatcher, not a substitute for licensed wildlife rehabilitators, and not affiliated with any specific wildlife rehabilitation center, government agency, or academic institution. The current public dataset is synthetic; that is documented on every surface. Wildlife911’s Virginia edition is curated reference content that recommends Virginia rehab options to a Virginia user. It does not dispatch help.

Responsibility

WildlifeStats is built by Michael Oak Advisors, an institutional consulting firm serving mission-driven investment organizations. The framework is an independent research initiative not associated with any client engagement. Architectural decisions, data calibration, and content curation are the responsibility of the firm; published critiques and limitations are documented openly in the project’s spec corpus.

Contact

For partnership inquiries (state wildlife agency contributions, partner rehab organization data sharing, academic collaboration), to express interest in a state edition of Wildlife911, or to report a content error: wildlifestats@michaeloak.com.

Status of the build

The public site is in active development. Phase 1 through Phase 6 ship the structural framework, data surface, section pages, and SEO baseline. Phases in flight include the partner data ingestion pipeline, the secure tier for credentialed researchers, the WREN AI assistant, the Wildlife911 LLM-driven pill, and the Flyway social-phenology early-warning pipeline. Each phase is documented in the repository’s handoff folder. Section pages note their build status individually.
