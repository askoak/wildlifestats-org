National Directory · Virginia

Wildlife rehabilitation centers in Virginia

7 verified licensed wildlife rehabilitation organizations in Virginia.

A.E.R.O.

Woodbridge · aeroanimalrescue.org

501(c)(3) organization dedicated to the rescue and rehabilitation of orphaned, injured, and sick native Virginia wildlife. Active in Fairfax, Prince William, Fauquier, and Stafford counties.

Blue Ridge Wildlife Center

Boyce · blueridgewildlifectr.org

To protect and conserve native wildlife by integrating veterinary medicine, rehabilitation, public education, professional training, and research.

Rockfish Wildlife Sanctuary

Shipman · rockfishwildlifesanctuary.org

At Rockfish Wildlife Sanctuary, every injured or orphaned wild animal gets the professional, compassionate care it deserves so it can return to the wild. Specializes in caring for clinically healthy orphaned wildlife.

Southwest Virginia Wildlife Center

Roanoke · swvawildlifecenter.org

Conserving Virginia's wildlife through rehabilitation and release while teaching care and respect of native species. One of only 3 wildlife veterinary hospitals in Virginia authorized to treat threatened and endangered species.

Wildlife Center of Virginia

Waynesboro · wildlifecenter.org

Teaching the world to care about, and care for, wildlife and the environment. Founded 1982; one of the world's premier teaching hospitals for wildlife and conservation medicine.

Wildlife Rescue League

Falls Church · www.wildliferescueleague.org

Non-profit 501(c)(3) organization dedicated to providing care for sick, injured, and orphaned wildlife with the intent of releasing them back into their native habitat. Serves Northern Virginia and surrounding counties.

Wildlife Response Inc.

Virginia Beach · www.wildliferesponse.org

Distinctive 501(c)(3) volunteer organization devoted solely to the care of orphaned, injured, and displaced native wildlife in the Hampton Roads region.
