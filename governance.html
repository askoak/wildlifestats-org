Governance

WildlifeStats organizes access to data in tiers. The public tier holds aggregate, suppression-aware figures available to anyone. Successive tiers — partner, research, and surveillance — carry progressively more detail and progressively stricter access controls, matched to the sensitivity of the data and the responsibilities of the recipient.

The framework is designed to accept data from many independent rehabilitation centers. A contributing center retains ownership of its records; the framework standardizes, aggregates, and publishes only what the governing tier permits. No contributing organization is named on the public tier without its consent, and no public figure can be traced to a single center or a single case.

This page will set out the data tier framework, the partnership model for centers that wish to contribute, and the privacy and suppression rules that govern each tier. Until those rules are finalized and published, the dataset shown across the site is synthetic.
