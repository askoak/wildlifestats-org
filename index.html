National Wildlife Rehabilitation Research Framework

WildlifeStats

WildlifeStats is a national research framework for the analysis of wildlife rehabilitation patient data. The current dataset is synthetic — 100,000 patient records generated from regional distribution models — and is intended to demonstrate the framework's methodology rather than report real admissions. The framework is built for researchers, policy analysts, educators, and conservation organizations who need a standardized, queryable view of wildlife intake patterns at national scale. Every analysis published here states its data provenance.

  • National Parks

  • Wildlife

  • Methodology

What this is

  • A research framework demonstrating standardized analysis of wildlife rehabilitation data at national scale.
  • A synthetic dataset — honestly labeled as such on every page that touches it.
  • A methodology for ingesting heterogeneous records from many centers into one queryable structure.

What this isn't

  • Not live or real-time admissions data.
  • Not a fundraising platform for any organization.
  • Not a replacement for state wildlife agencies or rehabilitator directories.
  • Not a partner self-service platform — yet. The partnership model is described under Governance.
