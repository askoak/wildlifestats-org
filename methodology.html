Methodology

Methodology

This page documents how the WildlifeStats dataset is constructed. The current dataset is synthetic: 100,000 patient records generated from regional distribution models rather than collected from operating centers. The structure mirrors what a real multi-center dataset would hold — species, taxonomic class, intake reason, county, season, and aggregate outcome — so that analyses developed against the synthetic data transfer directly to real data when partner centers contribute it.

Synthetic records are produced deterministically from a fixed random seed, so any published figure can be regenerated and audited. County-level distribution is modeled across all fifty states and the District of Columbia, weighted by population density as a proxy for encounter likelihood and by biogeographic region as a proxy for species composition. Seasonal patterns are modulated by latitude.

Outcome data is reported in aggregate only. No record on the public tier can be resolved to an individual case, and counts below a fixed suppression threshold are withheld. The full generation procedure, the regional species archetypes, and the suppression rules will be published here in detail as the framework reaches launch readiness.
